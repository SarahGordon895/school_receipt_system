CHAPTER 1 : INTODUCTION
  
1.1 GENERAL INTRODUCTION (MOTIVATION & BACKGROUND)  
In many secondary schools in Tanzania, fee management is still handled using manual methods such as receipt books, notebooks, and spreadsheets. These methods are time-consuming, error-prone, and make it difficult to keep accurate records. Communication about fees is often done verbally or through students, which is unreliable and may lead to misinformation.  
Parents are frequently not informed about fee balances, payment deadlines, or overdue amounts until problems arise. As schools grow in student population, managing fees manually becomes increasingly difficult. This project is motivated by the need for a simple digital solution that allows schools to track fees efficiently while enabling parents to view bills, make payments, and receive timely reminders using modern web technologies.  
  
  
1.2  STATEMENT OF THE PROBLEM  
Despite the increasing adoption of information systems in educational institutions, many secondary schools in Tanzania still rely on manual or semi-manual methods for managing student fee records. In these schools, fee management processes are characterized by poor data centralization, delayed communication with parents, and inefficient tracking of student payments. These challenges negatively affect school revenue management, administrative efficiency, and parent satisfaction.  
 
 
Financial Inefficiency: The lack of a centralized fee tracking system makes it difficult for schools to clearly identify paid and unpaid fees. Delayed updates of balances and penalties lead to revenue loss and poor financial planning.  
Administrative Overload: Manual recording of payments increases workload for school staff and leads to errors, missing records, and delayed updates of student fee information.  
Communication Gap: Parents often do not receive timely fee information and rely on students for updates, which can be inaccurate. The lack of automated reminders results in late payments and fee arrears.  
Lack of Transparency and Accessibility: Parents do not have real-time access to fee balances, payment history, or fee structures, causing misunderstandings and frequent visits to the school for clarification.  
 
In summary, the absence of an integrated, automated system results in delayed revenue cycles, high operational costs, and a poor user experience, necessitating a digital solution that synchronizes payment processing with automated reminders.  
  
1.3 PROJECT OBJECTIVES
 
MAIN OBJECTIVE  
To design and develop a web-based student fee tracking and reminder system that enables secondary schools to manage fee records efficiently and communicate payment information directly to parents through SMS and email.  
 
 
SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES 
To achieve the general goal, the project will focus on the following milestones: 
To perform a requirements analysis by identifying the specific financial data points, user roles, and notification triggers required for secondary school fee management in the Tanzanian context. 
To design a centralized MySQL database and system architecture that securely stores student profiles, parent contact information, and fee structures while maintaining data integrity. 
To develop a web-based back end using the Laravel framework capable of calculating real-time balances and processing digital transactions with automated timestamped receipts. 
To implement a responsive user interface and role-based access control that allows Admins to manage records and Parents to view financial history across both desktop and mobile devices. 
 
  
1.4 SIGNIFICANCE OF THE PROJECT 
The implementation of the Web-Based Fee Tracking & Reminder System carries significant value for the education sector in Tanzania: 
For School Management: ensure the school stuff to keep track of the students fee payment and collection via parents directly through SMS and Email alert, and not via student as the medium to get through to the parent. 
For Parents:  ensuring parents have direct access to the students fee portfolio and awareness on the Fee collection per the school administrative structure  
For Academic Continuity: By streamlining fee collection, ensuring that students' learning processes are not hindered by financial administrative delays. 
Technical Contribution: This project serves as a practical application of Laravel framework and SMS and Email integration to solve a community-level problem. 
 
1.5 STAKEHOLDERS DESCRIPTIONS 
Stakeholders are individuals or groups that have an interest in the Web-Based Fee Tracking & Reminder System or are affected by its development and use. The key stakeholders involved in this project are described below. 
Super Admin  
The Super Admin is typically the system developer or a high-level IT officer. Their role is Managing system updates, configuring the SMS/Email API gateways, performing database backups, and creating the initial accounts for School Admins. 
School Admin  
This role is usually assigned to the (Bursar, Headmaster, or Academic Master). They set the fee structures (e.g., how much tuition is for Form I vs. Form IV), register new students, and manually input payment data when a parent provides a bank slip. They are also responsible for generating the monthly reports that show who has paid and who hasn't.
 3. Parents/Guardians  
Parents are the external stakeholders. They use the system to check their child's current balance without having to travel to the school. Their primary "interaction" is often passive—receiving the automated SMS alerts that remind them of upcoming deadlines or confirming that a payment has been received. 
4. Students  
• While students may not have a login account in a basic fee system, The data managed by the system directly affects them. Accurate records ensure that a student isn't accidentally sent home for fees they have already paid. In more advanced versions, students might use the system to print their own "financial clearance" forms before exams. 
 


 CHAPTER 2: LITERATURE REVIEW 
 2.1 Introduction 
In Tanzania, many schools face loss of financial data of students leading to , confusion, overcrowding, and service inefficiencies, mainly due to manual recording of data and transaction made. Ineffective systems increase staff workload. This review focuses on educational management systems in Tanzanian schools. 
 
2.2 Overview of Educational Management Systems 
According to Al-Mamary (2020), the adoption of Management Information Systems (MIS) in educational institutions is no longer a luxury but a necessity for operational efficiency. Globally, schools have transitioned from paper-based ledgers to automated systems to manage student data.  However, many systems focus heavily on academic performance (grading) while neglecting the financial integration required for sustainable school operations 
 
2.3 The Impact of Automated Notifications on Payment Behavior 
A study in financial technology suggests that timely reminders significantly alter payment behavior. In the context of school fees, parents often miss deadlines not due to a lack of funds, but due to forgetfulness or a lack of clear information regarding the exact balance. By utilizing Short Message Service (SMS) and Email notifications, schools can provide a "nudge" that reduces late payments by an estimated 25–40%. 
 
2.4 Technologies Used 
Studies show that Web Based Fee Tracking & Reminder System use web-based platforms, databases for storing patient information, and mobile technologies such as SMS and Email notifications for reminders. 
2.5 Challenges in Existing Systems 
Existing fee management systems in many secondary schools are costly, they depend on manual processes, and lack effective communication with guardians. The absence of automated reminders leads to late payments, while limited system accessibility reduces transparency of fee information. Additionally, system complexity and limited technical skills among school staff make existing solutions difficult to use and poorly adopted. 

2.6 Research Gap 
Although these management systems improve educational and information efficiency in schools, Most Tanzanian schools still rely mostly in manual recording of transactions, since existing systems are expensive, complex or designed for bigger schools and afraid of sharing of confidential data. 
 
2.7 Summary 
This project fills the gap by providing a lightweight, focused, and cost-effective solution specifically designed for the fee-tracking and communication needs of Tanzanian secondary schools. 

  CHAPTER 3: METHODOLOGY 
The project will follow the Agile Methodology. This allows us to build the core fee-tracking engine first, then layer the notification services (SMS and Email) on top through iterative cycles. 
3.1 Requirement Gathering and Analysis 
We will identify the specific data points needed for communication: 
Contact Data: Ensuring the database captures both valid phone numbers for SMS and verified email addresses for digital receipts. 
Trigger Events: Defining when notifications should be sent  
3.2 System Design 
The design phase will focus on the bellow. 
Database Design:The parents able will include fields for phone_number and email_address. 
Logic Design: We will design a "Notification Controller" in Laravel that determines whether to send an SMS, an Email, or both, based on the urgency of the message. 
3.3 Implementation (Coding) 
The technical stack is expanded to handle dual-channel communication: 
Backend: Laravel. We will utilize Laravel's built-in Notification System, which allows sending messages across different "channels" simultaneously. 
SMS Integration: Using an external API to send text alerts. 
Email Integration: Configuring SMTP using services of Gmail servers to send professional fee statements and receipts. 
Database: MySQL to store the logs of sent messages to ensure no parent is notified twice for the same event.

Functional requirements.
Functionality requirements are capabilities and features that a system provides to fulfill its intended purpose or objectives. In the context of web-based fee tracking and reminder system, functionality would encompass all the operations and interactions that users can perform within the system. Such functionality is as listed below.
Student registration
The system shall allow the school admin to register new students by entering details such as student name, class, and parent information.
Fee structure management

Payment recording

Notification management
